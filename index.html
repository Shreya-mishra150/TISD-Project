Aquasol

Aquasol typically refers to a water-soluble material or technology designed to minimize environmental impact, often used in applications like labels, packaging, or even cleaning products, where dissolving completely in water reduces waste and promotes sustainability.Sustainability focus: The primary benefit of Aquasol is its ability to dissolve completely in water, eliminating the need for additional waste disposal and contributing to a more circular economy. Applications: Aquasol technology can be used in various applications like water-soluble labels for products, packaging materials that dissolve in water.

Kaarigiri

Project Karigari was established in 1997 with an aim to provide training and livelihood to marginalised women across India. The motto of the project is ‘Earn while you Learn’, which draws from Literacy India’s three pillars — Education, Empowerment, Employment. While education is at the core of our efforts to mainstream marginalised women, training them for jobs is equally important.Karigari believes that women and the youth are torch bearers of sustainable development. Their potential as agents of change in the lives of their children and their communities at large need to be harnessed

Footprintx

footprints refers to the environmental impact or measure of resource consumption associated with a particular technology, product, or activity, essentially indicating the "mark" it leaves on the planet, often measured in terms of carbon emissions, water usage, or land use throughout its lifecycle. Key points about "footprints": Ecological Footprint: The most common type of footprint, measuring the amount of biologically productive land and water required to sustain a person's lifestyle or an organization's operations. Carbon Footprint: Specifically refers to the amount of greenhouse gas emissions generated by an activity or product.
